1. Write down the full and overdamped Langevin equations of the Brownian particle, including the time correlation of the thermal noise.

Solution: The full Langevin equation:

\[ \frac{d^2x}{d{t^2}} + \gamma \frac{dx}{dt} + \omega_0^2 x = \frac{F(t)}{M} \]

The overdamped Langevin equation: \[ \gamma \frac{dx}{dt} + kx = \frac{F(t)}{M} \]

2. Write down the Fokker-Planck equation (FPE) for the probability distribution P(x, t) of the particle, corresponding to the overdamped Langevin equation.

Solution: The Fokker-Planck equation (FPE) \[ \frac{\partial P}{\partial t} = \frac{k}{\gamma} [\frac{\partial(xP)}{\partial x} + T\frac{\partial^2P}{\partial x^2}] \]

3. Get the steady-state solution of the FPE.

Solution: \[ P(x) = ( \frac {k}{2 \pi k_B T}) ^{\frac{1}{2}}exp(-\frac{kx^2}{2 k_B T}) \]
